Understand multi-element devices

Multi-element in MultiSense

A multi-element device measures multiple loads on a single device, for example the main electricity, a boiler and solar panels. In MultiSense these different loads are split over multiple logical devices named multi-element slave devices that only exist in the application and not in the field.

These slave devices will have a unique name, but all other attributes will be derived from their master multi-element device.

In the configuration of a device life cycle you can configure a pretransition check to whether there are still multi-element slaves linked to the multi-element device. Devices that are still linked with a slave cannot be decommissioned. See “Understand transitions” for more information.

Configuration

Three different configurations are possible:

  • Three phase device with three different outputs.

  • One phase device with two different outputs.

  • One phase device with only one output.
