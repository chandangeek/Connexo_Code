Time of use calendars

Understand time of use

Time of use (TOU) energy plans contain multiple rate periods rather than a single flat rate. With the implementation of smart meters, we can begin to understand how consumption patterns differ over the course of the day. For example, in residential contexts, standard consumption typically peaks in the morning when people wake up and again in the evening when they return home. Rate periods are linked to the observed change patterns in energy demand during specific periods. For example, peak demand periods result in increased electricity prices and emissions and in rewards for customers who shift their consumption off these peak periods. With the time of use energy plans, it is equally important to monitor when energy is used as how much it is used.

The time of use calendars in Connexo have a rule based format. Times are stored in UTC time. The indicated time zone is used to make the correct conversion.

The time of use calendars are imported and viewed in Connexo Admin. They are put into practice in Connexo MultiSense by a meter operator or meter expert. For the latter, reference is made to the Connexo MultiSense user guide.
